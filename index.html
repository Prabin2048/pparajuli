Dr. Prabin Parajuli

Postdoctoral Researcher

About Me

I am a theoretical physicist in quantum information sciences and condensed matter physics. I am originally from Nepal. I completed a Master's degree in physics from Tribhuvan University, Nepal in 2016. After that, I came to the University of California, Merced to pursue a Ph.D. degree in Physics. I have completed my Ph.D. degree in May 2022. From June 2022 to September 2023, I worked as a Postdoctoral researcher at the Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory. Since Dec 2023 I have worked as a Postdoctoral researcher at the University of Tennessee, Knoxville.

Research Interests

My research interest lies in the intersection of quantum many-body physics, quantum information science, and atomic, molecular, and optical physics. In my doctoral study, I worked on projects related to quantum many-body state preparation. I developed 3 different techniques for a fast and efficient many-body state preparation in Jaynes-Cummings lattices which are based on the nonlinear optical ramping method, shortcuts to adiabaticity method, and quantum optimal control method. In my first postdoctoral tenure, I worked on a novel color center qubit synthesis project and a quantum sensing project. Currently, I am working on a quantum spin-liquid project. Our essential goal is to realize the quantum spin liquid phase of 4-He in 1d geometry.
