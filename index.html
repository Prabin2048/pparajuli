Dr. Prabin Parajuli

About Me

I am a theoretical physicist working on quantum information sciences and condensed matter physics. I am originally from Nepal. I have completed a Master's degree in physics from the Tribhuvan University, Nepal in 2016. After that, I came to the University of California, Merced to persue Ph.D. degree in Physics. I have completed my Ph.D. degree in May 2022. From June 2022 to September 2023, I worked at the Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory as a Postdoctoral researcher. Since Dec 2023 I have been working at the University of Tennessee, Knoxville as a Postdoctoral researcher.

Research Interests

My research interest lies in the intersection of quantum manybody physics, quantum information sciencs and atomic, molecular and optical physics. In my doctoral study, I had worked on projects related to quantum many-body state preparation. I had developed 3 different techniques for a fast and efficient many body state preparation in Jaynes-Cummings lattices whcih are based on nonlinear optical ramping method, shortcuts to adiabaticity method, and quantum optimal control method. In my first postdoctoral tenure, I had worked on novel color center qubit synthesis project, and quantum sensing project. Now, I am working on quantum spin liquid project. Our essential goal is to realize quantum spin liquid phase of 4-He in 1d geometry.
